BREEDER

Readings are not written. They are bred.

Each analysis entry is a genome; each item (insight, tension, so-what, missing) is a codon. Themes are species. You don't write toward a goal — you mutate, recombine, and keep whatever turns out most interesting. The path to a strong reading rarely looks like the reading.

1
Pick a genome in the Library (browse by species). It becomes the parent.
2
Spawn a generation. Mutation swaps codons for their rhyme-neighbours from other entries — semantic drift, not noise. Each offspring is scored by novelty: how unlike everything seen.
3
Promote the interesting one. It becomes the new parent and enters your Pool with its full lineage. Or Cross… two genomes to splice their codons.
